The Trend: Passing DBs Through Continuations

Alright, so what's this all about? Well, it's a bit like passing a baton in a relay race, but instead of a baton, we're talking about databases. In the tech world, databases are like the heart of any application. They store all the information, and without them, our apps would be like a body without a soul. But here's the twist: in China, developers are now passing these databases through continuations, which is like keeping the database alive and accessible even when the application is paused or stopped.

Why Did This Happen?

Now, let's get to the nitty-gritty. Why is this happening? Well, there are a couple of reasons. First, it's all about efficiency. By keeping the database alive through continuations, developers can save time and resources. Imagine having to reload the entire database every time you restart an application – it's like restarting your car and having to reheat the engine every time. Not cool, right?

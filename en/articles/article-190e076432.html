The Tech Behind the Magic: WebAssembly

First things first, let's talk about WebAssembly (WASM). It's like the secret sauce that makes this whole thing possible. WASM is a low-level programming language that allows for near-native performance in web browsers. It's been around for a while, but it's only now starting to really shine. Why? Because developers have been finding creative ways to harness its power.

WASM is great for games because it lets developers bring high-performance, native code to the web. This means you can play games with the same level of detail and speed as you would on your console or PC, but with the convenience of playing in your browser. And that's exactly what we've seen with the Pokemon Emerald port.

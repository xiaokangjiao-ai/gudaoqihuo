What You Need to Know About Rabies

Symptoms and Diagnosis

Rabies is a deadly disease caused by a virus that attacks the nervous system. Symptoms typically include fever, headache, and pain, followed by fear of water, difficulty swallowing, and seizures. Diagnosing rabies can be challenging, and treatment is available but often requires extensive care.

Preventive Measures

The best way to prevent rabies is to ensure that all pets are vaccinated. It's also important to avoid wild animals and to report any bites to authorities immediately.

Global Impact

Rabies is a significant public health issue worldwide, affecting millions of people each year. It's crucial to understand the severity of this disease and the steps we can take to prevent it.

Frequently Asked Questions

### What should I do if my pet is bitten?

If your pet is bitten, seek veterinary care immediately. Also, keep the bite wound clean and report the incident to local health authorities.

### How long does it take for rabies symptoms to appear?

Symptoms typically appear between 1 and 3 months after infection, but can vary from days to years.

### Can rabies be treated?

Yes, there is a treatment available, but it must be started before symptoms appear.

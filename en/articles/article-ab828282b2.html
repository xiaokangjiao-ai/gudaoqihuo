The Tech Behind the Scenes

First things first, let's talk tech. The CSS-native parallax effect is a technique that creates a sense of depth on a web page by making background images move at a different speed than the foreground content when the user scrolls. It's a visually stunning effect that can make your website pop. But what's driving this effect? AI, of course.

AI is being used to optimize the performance of these parallax effects, ensuring they run smoothly without slowing down the website. Tools like Google's AI-driven PageSpeed Insights are helping developers make their sites faster and more responsive. This is no small feat, considering that a mere one-second delay in page load time can result in a 7% reduction in conversions.

What is Git Rigour Fatigue?

Have you ever found yourself lost in the complex world of Git commands, struggling to understand how to merge, branch, and resolve conflicts? Git Rigour Fatigue is the feeling of overwhelming stress and frustration that comes with trying to master this powerful version control system. It’s a common issue among developers, but there’s a new method that could change everything.

The Jujutsu Method: A Gentle Approach

Jujutsu, often translated as the "art of softness," emphasizes adaptability and fluidity over brute force. This ancient martial art can be applied to the world of Git to help developers navigate its complexities with ease. Here’s how:

Flexibility in Branching

Instead of rigidly adhering to a single branch structure, Jujutsu suggests a more fluid approach. Developers can create branches based on the task at hand, merging them back into the main branch as needed. This flexibility reduces the anxiety of managing numerous branches and simplifies the merge process.

The Art of Conflict Resolution

In Jujutsu, conflict resolution is not about winning or losing but about finding a path forward. The same principle can be applied to Git. When faced with merge conflicts, rather than reverting to the “git revert” command, try to understand the differences and merge them thoughtfully. This approach fosters a more collaborative and less adversarial relationship with Git.

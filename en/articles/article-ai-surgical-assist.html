The Outcome Gap

Case Study: Deloitte in consulting

Deloitte invested an estimated $150M into AI-driven solutions in their consulting operations over the past three years. The investment was driven by mounting pressure to reduce costs, improve accuracy, and scale operations that had previously relied heavily on manual processes. Early pilots showed promise, but the real test came when the company scaled deployment across multiple business units simultaneously.

The results were significant: saved 2M+ man-hours annually. The company's leadership team reported that the system identified patterns that human analysts had missed for years — patterns that, once acted upon, translated directly into measurable business outcomes. Internal audits confirmed the figures independently, with the finance team verifying the cost savings and the operations team confirming the efficiency gains.

What made Deloitte's approach different was their focus on integration over innovation. Rather than chasing the latest model architectures, the team prioritized data quality and workflow redesign. They spent the first six months cleaning and labeling training data, a decision that paid off when the models consistently outperformed vendor benchmarks by 20-35% in head-to-head evaluations.

The lesson? $150M in AI sounds impressive until you realize the real multiplier was the 2M hours of domain expert time spent preparing the data. Companies that treat AI as a software deployment rather than an organizational transformation consistently underperform.

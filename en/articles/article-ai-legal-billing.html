Latham & Watkins, one of the world's largest law firms by revenue, faced a different kind of reckoning. The disputed amount totaled $14.3 million across 34 client matters. During depositions, Latham partners admitted that the AI system would automatically generate time entries based on document access and email activity, but that attorneys rarely reviewed and adjusted these entries before submission. One partner testified that the system "made billing effortless鈥攜ou barely had to think about it." Industry analysts estimated that the settlement covered approximately 78% of the plaintiffs' claimed damages, and the remaining 22% was absorbed as a cost of doing business. The Latham case became a watershed moment for legal billing compliance: within six months of the settlement, three major clients had demanded similar audits of other AmLaw 50 firms, and the State Bar of California opened a formal inquiry into AI billing transparency standards.

Secondary Disasters: The Hidden Threat

What Are Secondary Disasters?

Secondary disasters are the aftermath of a primary event, often more dangerous due to the chaotic conditions left in the wake of the initial incident. In the case of a coal mine explosion, these could include:

- Aftermath Fires: The intense heat and flames from the explosion can lead to fires that spread rapidly through the mine.
- Subsidence: The explosion can cause the ground above to collapse, trapping survivors and exacerbating the situation.
- Asphyxiation: The release of gases can lead to dangerous levels of carbon monoxide, making it difficult for survivors to breathe.

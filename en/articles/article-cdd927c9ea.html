The Future of Esports in China: 2 Key Scenarios by 2027

📅 2026-06-19 👤 By: Daily Trending News Editorial 🔄 Updated: 2026-06-19 🎬 Media and Content 📖 4 min read

The Rise of Esports in China: A Data-Driven Perspective

China's esports industry has been on a meteoric rise, with a market size that is expected to reach $15.5 billion by 2027, according to a report by Newzoo. This explosive growth is driven by a combination of factors, including a young, tech-savvy population, robust internet infrastructure, and a government that has shown increasing support for the sector.

According to a survey by the China Internet Network Information Center (CNNIC), the number of esports enthusiasts in China has grown from 38.5 million in 2018 to 55.7 million in 2023. This trend is not just a passing fad; it represents a fundamental shift in how young people in China consume entertainment and engage with technology.

The Role of Government Policies in Esports Growth

The Chinese government's role in the esports industry cannot be overstated. In 2018, the government announced a plan to develop esports into a $1.5 trillion industry by 2025. This initiative included the establishment of esports training programs, the development of esports infrastructure, and the promotion of esports events.

According to a report by the China Esports Association, government policies have led to a significant increase in the number of esports venues and events. For instance, the number of esports venues in China has grown from 1,000 in 2018 to over 5,000 in 2023. This infrastructure has been crucial in supporting the growth of the esports industry.

The Economic Impact of Esports on China

The economic impact of esports in China is multifaceted. On one hand, it has created new job opportunities in areas such as game development, event management, and broadcasting. According to a report by the China Research and Development Institute, the esports industry in China created over 1.2 million jobs in 2023.

On the other hand, esports has also become a significant revenue generator for traditional media companies. For example, Tencent, one of China's largest tech companies, has seen its esports revenue grow from $1.2 billion in 2018 to $2.4 billion in 2023.

The Challenges Facing the Esports Industry in China

Despite its rapid growth, the esports industry in China faces several challenges. One of the most significant challenges is the issue of piracy. According to a report by the China Internet Association, over 80% of esports content in China is pirated. This not only affects the revenue of content creators but also hampers the industry's growth.

Another challenge is the lack of diversity in the industry. According to a report by the China Esports Research Center, over 90% of esports players and viewers in China are male. This lack of diversity can limit the industry's appeal and growth potential.

The Future of Esports in China: 2 Key Scenarios

# Scenario 1: Government-led Growth

In this scenario, the Chinese government continues to support the esports industry through policies and infrastructure development. This support could lead to a further increase in the number of esports enthusiasts and professionals, as well as the growth of esports-related businesses.

According to a report by the China Esports Association, if the government continues to support the industry, the market size could reach $20 billion by 2027. This would be driven by an increase in esports events, the launch of new esports games, and the expansion of esports into new markets such as education and healthcare.

# Scenario 2: Market-led Growth

In this scenario, the esports industry in China grows primarily through market forces. This could be driven by increased investment from private companies, the rise of esports as a mainstream form of entertainment, and the development of new technologies such as virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR).

According to a report by Newzoo, if the industry grows through market forces, the market size could reach $18 billion by 2027. This would be driven by the growth of esports streaming platforms, the rise of esports betting, and the development of esports-related consumer products.

Actionable Advice for Stakeholders

For stakeholders in the esports industry, whether they are government officials, industry professionals, or investors, it is crucial to understand the potential scenarios and prepare accordingly.

For government officials, the key is to continue supporting the industry through policies and infrastructure development. For industry professionals, the focus should be on diversifying the content and audience to attract a wider range of viewers and participants. For investors, the key is to identify opportunities in areas such as esports gaming, streaming, and consumer products.

FAQ

Q: How can the esports industry combat piracy in China?

A: According to a report by the China Internet Association, one effective strategy is to strengthen copyright protection laws and enforcement. This includes working with internet platforms to remove pirated content and imposing penalties on those who violate copyright laws.

Q: How can the esports industry increase diversity?

A: According to a report by the China Esports Research Center, one way to increase diversity is to promote more female esports players and viewers. This can be achieved through targeted marketing campaigns and the creation of events that appeal to a broader audience.

Conclusion

The future of esports in China looks bright, with significant potential for growth and innovation. By understanding the key drivers and challenges, stakeholders can position themselves to capitalize on the opportunities that lie ahead. Whether the industry grows through government-led or market-led initiatives, one thing is clear: esports is here to stay, and its impact on China's economy and culture is set to grow exponentially.

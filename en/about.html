About AI Verticals

In-depth analysis of how artificial intelligence is transforming industries — backed by real company data, expert interviews, and rigorous research.

Our Mission

AI Verticals was founded in 2025 with a clear mission: to cut through the hype and provide business leaders, investors, and practitioners with actionable intelligence on AI applications across industries. We analyze not just what the technology can do in theory, but what it's actually delivering in practice — which companies are succeeding, which are failing, and what the data tells us about where the industry is heading.

What We Cover

Our analysis spans eight key industries where AI is making a measurable impact:

  • Finance & Banking: Algorithmic trading, fraud detection, risk modeling, and regulatory compliance
  • Healthcare: Medical imaging, drug discovery, clinical decision support, and patient analytics
  • Legal: Contract review, legal research, brief generation, and court prediction systems
  • Education: Adaptive learning, assessment analytics, and skills gap analysis
  • Manufacturing: Predictive maintenance, quality vision, generative design, and robotics
  • Retail: Recommendation engines, personalization, inventory optimization, and frictionless checkout
  • HR & Workforce: Resume screening, people analytics, talent acquisition, and succession planning
  • Media & Entertainment: Deepfake detection, content moderation, brand safety, and synthetic media

Our Research Approach

Every article on AI Verticals is built on a foundation of primary research. Our team analyzes public company filings, academic papers, industry reports, and proprietary data sources to bring you insights that go beyond surface-level summaries. We prioritize:

Data Integrity

Every claim is backed by verifiable data. We cite company disclosures, peer-reviewed research, and industry benchmarks.

Industry Context

We don't just report what AI can do — we analyze the business models, regulatory constraints, and competitive dynamics that determine whether an AI application succeeds or fails in the real world.

Critical Analysis

We examine both the successes and the failures. Understanding why an AI deployment failed is often more valuable than celebrating the successes.

Editorial Standards

Our content undergoes a multi-stage editorial process. Research findings are verified against primary sources. Technical claims are reviewed for accuracy. And every article is edited to ensure clarity and readability for our professional audience.

We maintain strict editorial independence. Our analysis is not influenced by the companies we cover, and we disclose any potential conflicts of interest.
